Le Brutaliste

Le Brutaliste is a mobile game I worked on at NAD, made in Unreal 4 with a team of 21.
This is an auto-scrolling platformer where you play as Howard, an angry architect in 1940s Montreal. Tap and swipe as Howard runs forward to destroy as much of your rival's creations as you can!

It is available on Google Play Store for free.

My tasks:

Level design on 3 stages, game design, menu navigation, trailer

Goals:

  • Build levels that fit the difficulty curve of the game.
  • Reward observation by introducing enemy patterns and then breaking them.
  • Reward execution by making the top path the best solution but also the hardest.

Video reflects the work of the entire team.

Process

The basic rules of the game were already decided before I joined the project. My tasks were to:

  • "Find the fun" and balance different aspects of the game to make it satisfying.
  • Establish the ideal metrics of a level using playtests.
  • Document rules of a level design for others to follow.

Metrics sheet, showing important scales after early playtesting, to help other level designers and me to build levels that followed the same rules, and to give modelers guidelines on model sizes.
